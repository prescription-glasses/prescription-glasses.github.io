What Do All Those Numbers Mean? understanding your glasses prescription

Understanding SPH, CYL, Axis, and Add Values

The first crucial component is SPH (Sphere), indicating the lens power needed for distance or near vision. A minus sign means you're nearsighted (myopia), while a plus sign signifies farsightedness (hyperopia). For those with astigmatism, you'll also see CYL (Cylinder) and Axis values. CYL measures the lens power for astigmatism, and Axis specifies the orientation of that corrective power on the lens.

Beyond these, an Add value may be present for individuals requiring multifocal lenses, like bifocals or progressive lenses. This "addition" power helps with reading or close-up tasks. Interpreting these details correctly ensures your new eyeglasses provide optimal clarity, whether you're looking for everyday wear or trendy oversized prescription glasses for a modern look.
