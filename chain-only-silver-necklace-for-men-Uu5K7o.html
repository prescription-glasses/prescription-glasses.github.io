  • **OD (Oculus Dexter)**: This Latin term refers to your right eye.

  • **SPH (Sphere)**: Indicates the main lens power, measured in **diopters**, required to correct either nearsightedness (indicated by a minus sign, -) or farsightedness (indicated by a plus sign, +).

  • **PRISM**: Less commonly seen, this value corrects vision problems related to eye alignment issues, helping to ensure both eyes work together effectively.

  • **Bifocal Lenses:** As we age, many people develop presbyopia, a condition where the eye's natural lens stiffens, making it difficult to focus on close objects. Bifocal **corrective lenses** address this by incorporating two distinct powers: one for distance vision in the top portion and a segment for near vision at the bottom. A visible line typically separates these two zones. This design helps people manage both far and near tasks without switching between different pairs of **eyewear**.
  • **Progressive Lenses:** Also known as no-line bifocals, progressive **optical lenses** offer a seamless transition between multiple vision powers – distance, intermediate (e.g., computer screens), and near. There's no visible line, providing a more aesthetic and natural visual experience. These **vision aids** are highly versatile for those with presbyopia, allowing for clear vision at all distances with a single pair of **prescription spectacles**.
