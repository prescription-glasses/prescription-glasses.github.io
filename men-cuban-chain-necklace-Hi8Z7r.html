Essential Preparations: What You Need Before You Shop

Before you even start browsing frames, a few crucial pieces of information are required. These preparations are key to ensuring your new online eyewear perfectly corrects your vision and fits comfortably.

Obtaining Your Valid Prescription

The absolute first step to buying prescription glasses on line is having a current and valid prescription from a licensed optometrist. Your prescription provides all the necessary details for lens crafting, including sphere (SPH), cylinder (CYL), axis, and sometimes an add power for multifocal lenses. Without this, online optical stores cannot accurately fulfill your order. Always ensure your prescription is up-to-date, preferably within the last year, to guarantee optimal vision correction. If you need an updated prescription, many online optometrist services now offer virtual eye exams for convenience.

Understanding Your Pupillary Distance (PD)

Your Pupillary Distance, or PD, is a critical measurement that often gets overlooked but is vital for accurate lens placement. It's the distance in millimeters between the centers of your pupils. An incorrect PD can lead to discomfort, eye strain, and blurred vision, even if your prescription is accurate. Many eye doctors include this on your prescription, but if not, you can measure it yourself using a ruler and a mirror. Some online glasses retailers, like 39 Dollar Glasses, offer helpful online tools such as AccuPD to assist with your pupillary distance measurement online, making the process simple and precise.
